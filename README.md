# Kudoku-Shrine-Website-P-1
📖 Project Description  KODOKU is an atmospheric, data-driven digital archive tracking the quiet intersection of sacred Japanese architecture and natural reclamation. Rather than treating abandoned Shinto shrines as static monuments to be artificially frozen in time, this collective documents them as living poetry—capturing their transition from human sacred spaces back into forest soil. The platform serves as a visual elegist for sanctuaries vanishing across high-humidity valleys and forgotten mountain ranges.  🌿 Key Focus Areas Decay Analytics: Tracks precise structural metrics to monitor deterioration before complete environmental integration.  Geospatial Mapping: Cross-references exact coordinates with an interactive mapping interface to monitor active regional stability risks.  Drone Photogrammetry: Integrates cutting-edge drone assets and living video loops to preserve a permanent 3D spatial atmosphere of the sites.  Sociological Context: Links physical degradation to historical research, tracking how Showa Era decentralization and regional logging shifts contributed to site abandonment.  🛠️ Design & Frontend Philosophy  Built in accordance with traditional Japanese editorial presentation principles, the frontend architecture uses a highly immersive user interface. It utilizes fluid, asymmetric grid layouts, smooth scroll-reveal triggers, a custom cursor-ring system, and editorial typography driven by Figtree, Nunito, Junge, and Yuji Mai font pairings. Cinematic HTML5 background video wrappers serve as digital windows directly into active field expeditions, creating a deeply respectful, solemn user experience.  📜 Operational Framework  All research is bound by strict internal guidelines: a rigid safety framework to navigate subsurface landslips and imminent structural failures, combined with a strict Leave No Trace policy to protect the organic integration of every site.
